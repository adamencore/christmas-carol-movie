I The Logline

On Christmas Eve, a miserly businessman is visited by three spirits who show him the cost of a life unlived.

A stop-motion animated musical featuring sixteen original songs that transform Scrooge's redemption into a family tradition worth returning to every December.

II The Story

A familiar tale, told with restraint, music, and heart.

Act One

The Miser

Scrooge is shape and shadow — face unrevealed until his name is spoken. He mocks Cratchit's gift, refuses Fred's dinner, and condemns the poor. Marley appears in chains.

Act Two

The Ghosts

Past, a child who remembers everything. Present, loud and jovial. Future, silent — he only points. Each shows Scrooge a truth he cannot unsee.

Act Three

Redemption

Christmas morning: his life is still his to live. He raises Bob's salary, buys the biggest turkey in the shop, and becomes the man his sister always believed he could be.

III The Cast

A small ensemble, drawn faithfully from Dickens.

IV Color & Atmosphere

Color follows Scrooge — cold, warm, dead, alive.

V The Music

These songs aren't decoration. They are the story.

Sixteen original songs by Adam Record — folksy, nostalgic, deeply story-driven. Not Broadway spectacle. A child will remember "Love Everywhere." A parent will cry at "The Home I Never Had."

VII Why It Lasts
